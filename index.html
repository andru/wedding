In case you haven't heard, we're getting hitched!

And we want to revel in our love for each other, and for you!

Please join us for a simple, earthy, and joyful ceremony with delicious wholesome vegan grub and an afternoon of sincere sillyness followed by live music, dancing, and debauchery from dusk to dawn at our friends' spectacularly beautiful farm in North Cornwall (near Launceston).

There's plenty of room for vans and camping, or there's accomodation in the area if camping's not your bag (we'd advise booking sooner rather than later though).

The Ceremony will start at 2PM for those of you driving on the day. Campers (and helpers!) welcome from Friday afternoon.

Location

Our friends are hosting us at their beautiful farm near Launceston in Cornwall.

Trekenner Farm, Launceston, PL15 9PH
What3Words: messaging.owls.thumps
Coords: 50.5782099 -4.3432144
Google Maps

The address will take you to the front entrance, so please use the coordinates or what3words to get to the rear gate, where you'll find parking and signage to the ceremony!

The house by the back gate is not a part of the farm, but there is a track to it's right which leads into the parking/camping field.

On The Day...

The hand-tying ceremony starts at 2PM, followed by dinner. After we've eaten there'll be an open mic where you're invited to say a few words, share a story, read a poem or sing a song. If you don't want to get up infront of everyone, there will be a memory book out to leave your thoughts, doodles, photos and keepsakes.

We'll have some evening nibbles out, and bands and DJs from about 8PM until late.

Parking will be on a field.

No dogs - sorry!

What to wear...

Wear whatever makes you feel fancy. From black tie and tails to spandex and glitter

Just make sure your footwear is field-ready, and bring a coat and a brolly...

What to bring...

Other than your fine selves, there are a few things we need you to bring!

  • A cup or mug for your drinks
  • Bring your own booze - we're putting on a margarita bar, but after that there's no bar and no shops to stock up!
  • Everything you need for your stay (there are no shops nearby!). There'll be dinner on Saturday, and breakfast on Sunday.
  • Farm-appropriate footwear
  • A brolly and a coat (just incase)
  • A song, poem, story, or wisdom to share at the open mic (if you want to!)
 

Where to stay...

If you're in a van or happy camping, join us in the camping field! There's running water and compost toilets, but bring everything else you need to camp.

Otherwise, there's lots of accomodation nearby. We can recommend Spring Park just round the corner, who have quirky shepherds huts and cabins, or Honicombe Holiday Village who are a bit further away but also have lots of cabins.
